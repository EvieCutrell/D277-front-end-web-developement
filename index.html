Overview of Washington State

Washington State is the westernmost state in the Pacific North-West (PNW) and of the contiguous United States. It was admitted to the Union in 1889 as the 42nd state.

The state spans an area of 71,362 square miles making it the 18th largest state in the union, and has a population count of over 7.8 million people; ranking it as the 13th most populous state. The borders the Pacific Ocean on its West, Canada to its North, Idaho on its East, and Oregon on its South. The state is well known for its variety of habitats including: the Cascades Range, which includes Mount Rainer, Mount Saint Helens, and the Olympic Mountains, Pudget Sound and Juan de Fuca Straight along with the Pacific Ocean, a wide variety of national forests, parks, and rainforests, as well as a couple deserts in Western Washington, west of the Cascades.


The flag of Washington State has the state's seal, First President George Washington on a blue background with a yellow ring that says "The Seal of the State of Washington 1889" emcompassing it, on top of a solid green flag. Washington State has been the birthing place of several large companies including: Amazon, Microsoft, Starbucks, Boeing, Costco, Alaska Airlines, Holland America, and UPS. Washington also holds strong ties to powerful labor unions and governmental work.
